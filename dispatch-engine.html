Engine overview
Two layers. One cost function.
The dispatch engine has a single goal: find the duty with the lowest total incremental cost for every trip, instantly, and keep improving the plan as the world changes. It never searches for the "best" driver — it searches for the duty whose total cost vector is lowest.
Layer 1
Instant Assignment
Fires the moment a booking is confirmed. Runs hard feasibility filters across all eligible duties, then scores every passing duty on the full cost function. The lowest-cost duty is assigned immediately — even 30 days in advance. No waiting, no human in the loop.
Layer 2
Continuous Re-Optimisation
Runs in the background on 10 event triggers. Reschedules the entire plan — 1-way and round trip legs in Pass 1, hourly bookings in Pass 2 — and only applies changes if the saving exceeds the configurable threshold. Immovable: trips within the 90-minute lock horizon.

Duty
The dispatch unit
The engine assigns trips to duties — not to drivers or vehicles individually. A duty is the only entity that can receive a booking. All cost scoring runs against the duty's current state.
Booking lifecycle
REQUESTED → PAID → CONFIRMED → ASSIGNED → EN ROUTE → IN TRIP → COMPLETED

Step 2
Cost scoring — lowest total wins
Every duty passing hard filters is scored. The engine picks the duty with the lowest total incremental cost. All terms are in local currency. Credits reduce cost; penalties increase it.

Total Assignment Cost

Duty with lowest total is assigned. Re-optimisation only applies if saving exceeds threshold.

=cost_per_empty_km × empty_km_to_pickup dead km from current position to trip pickup
+cost_per_empty_km × downstream_empty_km × downstream_weight dead km from dropoff to driver's next booking
+overtime_rate_per_min × overtime_minutes_added extra minutes beyond shift end caused by this trip
−idle_value_per_min × idle_minutes_filled negative cost — filling idle time is a gain
+lateness_penalty_per_min × minutes_late_at_pickup zero if on time; cannot exceed hard filter max
+churn_penalty re-optimisation only — zero on new bookings
−home_base_return_credit if trip moves displaced vehicle closer to home base
+end_of_shift_repositioning_cost second half of shift only — grows toward shift end
Cost
Empty km to pickupDead driving from driver's current position to pickup. Covers fuel/energy, vehicle wear, driver paid time while empty.
Cost
Downstream dead kmDead driving from this dropoff to driver's next confirmed booking. Zero if no next trip. Weight = 1.0 standard, 0.5 hourly (uncertain end location).
Penalty
Overtime minutesExtra minutes beyond shift end this assignment causes. Multiplied by overtime premium rate. Hard cap: 60 min per shift.
Credit
Idle minutes filledNegative term — filling idle time reduces total cost. Incentivises dense scheduling over sparse gaps.
Penalty
Lateness minutesMinutes late at pickup, within the allowed maximum (default 5 min). Zero if on time. Cannot exceed hard filter threshold.
Re-opt only
Churn penaltyFixed cost per re-assignment. Prevents re-optimisation from reshuffling trips for marginal gains. Zero on new bookings.
Credit
Home base return creditApplied when trip dropoff is closer to the vehicle's home base than its current position. Incentivises natural repositioning.
Penalty
End-of-shift repositioningWeight = 0 in first half of shift. Grows from 0 to max from midpoint to final hour. Maximum in final hour. No trip is ever blocked by this term alone.
End-of-shift repositioning weight curve
No trip is ever blocked because of this term. If a distant trip is the only feasible option, it is still assigned — it simply carries higher cost that the engine weighs against all alternatives.
Layer 2
Re-optimisation engine
Runs continuously in the background. Fires on 10 event types. Reschedules the full plan and only applies changes that exceed the saving threshold. The 90-minute lock horizon protects near-departure trips from disruption.
Event triggers — fire immediately
01New booking confirmed
02Booking cancelled
03Driver running late
04Vehicle added or returned to fleet
05Vehicle removed — planned
06Vehicle breakdown — urgent (+ ops escalation)
07Maintenance scheduled or complete
08Driver absence
09Charger offline or restored (EV re-check)
10City wait window expired
Health-check cron — every 15–30 min Safety net only. Detects gradual ETA drift and silent event failures. Not an optimisation trigger.
Engine passes
Pass 1 — 1-way & round trip
Re-stitch all 1-way and round trip legs. Full endpoint confidence. Global cost minimisation against the full cost function.
Pass 2 — Hourly bookings
Place hourly bookings into remaining gaps after Pass 1 completes. Conservative. High churn penalty prevents excessive reshuffling of hourly blocks.
Constraints
90 min lockTrips within 90 minutes of departure are immovable. Re-optimisation cannot touch them.
save thresholdReassignment only applied if saving exceeds configurable minimum. Prevents marginal reshuffling.
ON HOLDAfter each run, engine promotes ON HOLD bookings where capacity was freed.
churn penaltyFixed cost per reassignment in the scoring function. Prevents oscillation between near-equal solutions.
